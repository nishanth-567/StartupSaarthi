Q: What is the definition of a startup under Startup India?
A: A startup is an entity incorporated or registered in India not prior to 10 years, with annual turnover not exceeding ₹100 crore in any preceding financial year, working towards innovation, development, deployment or commercialization of new products, processes or services driven by technology or intellectual property.
Q: What are the tax benefits available to startups?
A: Startups can avail: (1) Income tax exemption for 3 consecutive years out of first 10 years, (2) Exemption from capital gains tax under Section 54EE and 54GB, (3) Exemption from tax on investments above fair market value (Angel Tax exemption), (4) Tax holiday for eligible startups.
Q: How does the Fund of Funds work?
A: The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) with a corpus of ₹10,000 crore is managed by SIDBI. It doesn't invest directly in startups but invests in SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs), who then invest in startups. This creates a multiplier effect for startup funding.
Q: What IPR support is available for startups?
A: Startups get: (1) 80% rebate on patent filing fees, (2) 50% rebate on trademark fees, (3) Panel of facilitators to assist in filing, (4) Government bears facilitator fees, (5) Fast-track examination of patent applications.
Q: What is SPICe+ form?
A: SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus) is an integrated web form for incorporating a company. It combines multiple services: Name reservation, Incorporation, DIN allotment, PAN, TAN, EPFO, ESIC, Profession Tax, and Opening of Bank Account - all in one form.
Q: What is AGILE form?
A: AGILE (Application for GST, EPFO, ESIC, Profession Tax) is linked with SPICe+ and enables startups to apply for various registrations simultaneously: GST, EPFO (Employees' Provident Fund), ESIC (Employees' State Insurance), and Professional Tax registration.
Q: What is Section 80IAC?
A: Section 80IAC provides income tax exemption to eligible startups for 3 consecutive assessment years out of first 10 years. Startup must be certified by Inter-Ministerial Board, incorporated after April 1, 2016, and engaged in innovation/development of new products or services.
Q: What is composition scheme under GST?
A: Composition scheme is for small taxpayers with turnover up to ₹1.5 crore. Benefits: Pay tax at flat rate (1% for manufacturers, 5% for restaurants, 6% for other suppliers), file quarterly returns, reduced compliance. Limitations: No input tax credit, can't do inter-state supply, can't supply through e-commerce.
Q: When is EPF registration mandatory?
A: EPF registration is mandatory for establishments employing 20 or more persons. Both employee and employer contribute 12% of basic salary + DA. Employees earning more than ₹15,000/month can opt out. Registration must be done within 1 month of crossing 20 employees threshold.
Q: When is ESI registration mandatory?
A: ESI registration is mandatory for establishments with 10 or more employees (20 in some states). Applicable to employees earning up to ₹21,000/month. Employee contributes 0.75% and employer contributes 3.25% of wages. Provides medical and cash benefits.
Q: What is a lead investor?
A: A lead investor is the primary investor in a funding round who takes charge of negotiations, sets terms, conducts due diligence, and often contributes the largest portion of capital. Other investors typically follow the lead investor's terms. Lead investors usually get board seats and special rights.
Q: What is a down round?
A: A down round is a funding round where shares are sold at a lower valuation than the previous round. This is dilutive for existing shareholders and can trigger anti-dilution provisions. Often happens during market downturns or when startup hasn't met growth targets.
Q: What is a flat round?
A: A flat round is when a startup raises funding at the same valuation as the previous round. Neither up nor down. Usually happens when startup needs capital but hasn't grown enough to justify higher valuation. Less dilutive than down round.
Q: What is participating preferred stock?
A: Participating preferred stock allows investors to get their liquidation preference first, then also participate in remaining proceeds with common shareholders. This is 'double dipping' - investors get paid twice. Can be capped (1x, 2x) or uncapped. Favorable to investors.
Q: What is a drag-along right?
A: Drag-along rights allow majority shareholders to force minority shareholders to join in the sale of a company. If majority agrees to sell, minority must sell their shares on same terms. Protects against minority blocking acquisition. Common in VC deals.
Q: What is a tag-along right?
A: Tag-along rights allow minority shareholders to join when majority shareholders sell their stake. If majority sells, minority can sell their shares on same terms. Protects minority from being left with illiquid shares. Opposite of drag-along.
Q: What is the Rule of 40?
A: Rule of 40 states that a SaaS company's growth rate + profit margin should be at least 40%. Example: 30% growth + 10% profit margin = 40%. Shows balance between growth and profitability. Used by investors to evaluate SaaS companies.
Q: What is T2D3?
A: T2D3 (Triple, Triple, Double, Double, Double) is a growth framework for SaaS startups to reach $100M ARR. Triple ARR in year 1 and 2, then double in years 3, 4, and 5. Example: $2M → $6M → $18M → $36M → $72M → $144M ARR.
Q: What is magic number in SaaS?
A: Magic Number = (Current Quarter ARR - Previous Quarter ARR) / Previous Quarter Sales & Marketing Spend. Measures sales efficiency. >1 is excellent (efficient growth), 0.75-1 is good, <0.5 means spending too much on acquisition. Helps optimize CAC.
Q: What is net revenue retention (NRR)?
A: NRR measures revenue retained from existing customers including expansions, upgrades, and downgrades. Formula: (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn - Contraction) / Starting ARR × 100. >100% is excellent (expansion revenue exceeds churn). Key metric for SaaS companies.
Q: What is gross margin?
A: Gross Margin = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue × 100. Shows profitability of core business before operating expenses. SaaS companies should aim for 70-80%+. Product companies typically 40-60%. Higher is better as more revenue available for growth.
