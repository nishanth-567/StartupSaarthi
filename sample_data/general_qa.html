Q: What is the difference between a Private Limited Company and an LLP?
A: A Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd) is a separate legal entity with limited liability for shareholders, requiring minimum 2 directors and 2 shareholders. It's ideal for startups seeking VC funding as investors prefer equity investments. An LLP (Limited Liability Partnership) combines partnership flexibility with limited liability, requiring minimum 2 partners. LLPs have fewer compliance requirements and lower costs but may face challenges in raising VC funding since investors cannot hold equity shares. Pvt Ltd is preferred for scalable startups planning to raise funding, while LLP suits professional services firms and consultancies.
Keywords: private limited, llp, company structure, incorporation, legal entity
Q: Can a foreign national start a company in India?
A: Yes, foreign nationals can incorporate a company in India. However, for a Private Limited Company, at least one director must be a resident of India (staying for a total period of not less than 182 days during the financial year). The incorporation process is fully online, so physical presence is not required. Foreign nationals need to provide their passport as identity proof and follow FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) guidelines for foreign direct investment.
Keywords: foreign national, fdi, company registration, fema
Q: What is the minimum capital requirement to start a company in India?
A: There is NO minimum capital requirement for Private Limited Companies and One Person Companies (OPC) in India. You can start with as little as ₹1,000 as authorized capital. However, you should consider the practical capital needed for your business operations, licenses, and initial expenses. The authorized capital determines the stamp duty you'll pay during incorporation.
Keywords: minimum capital, authorized capital, company registration
Q: What is Startup India and what benefits does it provide?
A: Startup India is a flagship initiative launched by the Government of India in January 2016 to build a robust startup ecosystem. Benefits include: (1) Tax exemptions - Income tax exemption for 3 years if certified by Inter-Ministerial Board, (2) Easier compliance - Self-certification under 6 labour and 3 environment laws for 3-5 years, (3) Funding support - Access to Fund of Funds (₹10,000 crore corpus) and Seed Fund Scheme (₹945 crore), (4) IPR benefits - 80% rebate on patent filing fees and fast-track examination, (5) Networking - Access to mentorship programs and investor connects.
Keywords: startup india, dpiit, government schemes, benefits, tax exemption
Q: What are the eligibility criteria for Startup India registration?
A: To register under Startup India (DPIIT recognition), your entity must: (1) Be incorporated as a Private Limited Company, Registered Partnership Firm, or Limited Liability Partnership (LLP), (2) Not be more than 10 years old from date of incorporation, (3) Have annual turnover not exceeding ₹100 crores in any previous financial year, (4) Be working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/services with potential to generate employment or create wealth, (5) Not be formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business. The registration is free and done online through the Startup India portal.
Keywords: eligibility, dpiit recognition, startup india registration, criteria
Q: What is the difference between equity and debt financing?
A: Equity Financing: You raise capital by selling ownership shares (equity) in your company to investors. Investors become shareholders and may get voting rights. You don't have to repay the money, but you dilute your ownership. Best for high-growth startups that need capital without repayment pressure. Debt Financing: You borrow money that must be repaid with interest, without giving up equity. You retain full ownership and control, but have repayment obligations regardless of business performance. Best for established startups with predictable cash flows. Many startups use a mix of both.
Keywords: equity financing, debt financing, funding types, investment
Q: What is a term sheet and what should I look for?
A: A term sheet is a non-binding document outlining the key terms and conditions of an investment deal between a startup and investors. Key clauses to review: (1) Valuation - Pre-money and post-money valuation determining your equity stake, (2) Investment amount - Capital being invested, (3) Liquidation preference - How proceeds are distributed if company is sold, (4) Anti-dilution provisions - Protection for investors if future rounds are at lower valuation, (5) Board composition - Investor representation on board, (6) Vesting schedule - Founder equity vesting terms, (7) Pre-emptive rights - Investor's right to participate in future rounds. Always have a lawyer review before signing.
Keywords: term sheet, investment terms, valuation, liquidation preference
Q: What documents do I need to register a Private Limited Company?
A: Required documents: (1) Identity Proof - PAN Card for Indian nationals, Passport for foreign nationals, (2) Address Proof - Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Driving License, or Bank Statement (not older than 2 months), (3) Passport-sized photographs of all directors and shareholders, (4) Registered office proof - Utility bill (electricity/water) not older than 2 months, rent agreement if rented, and NOC from property owner, (5) Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for all directors, (6) Director Identification Number (DIN) for all directors, (7) Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA) - drafted during incorporation process.
Keywords: company registration, documents required, incorporation, pvt ltd
Q: Do I need GST registration for my startup?
A: GST registration is MANDATORY if: (1) Your annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs for goods or ₹20 lakhs for services (₹10 lakhs for special category states), (2) You're involved in inter-state supply of goods or services (regardless of turnover), (3) You're an e-commerce operator or selling through e-commerce platforms, (4) You're involved in import/export, (5) You're a casual taxable person or non-resident taxable person. GST registration is VOLUNTARY if your turnover is below the threshold and you want to claim input tax credit or appear more credible to clients. Register online through the GST portal (gst.gov.in).
Keywords: gst registration, tax compliance, turnover threshold
